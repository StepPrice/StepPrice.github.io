Opinions on the future of AI

Bigger hands

By Sam Fogarty, a futurist enthusiast

The creation of Strong AI will bring about another revolution as profound as the industrial revolution, and so the human experience will change forever.

When we invented electricity, and utilised it with machinery, I think of that as giving our society ‘bigger hands’. Now one person can do the work of many because they have a machine that acts like a giant shovel, for example.

In the AI revolution, which we are approximately within right now, we are creating something that thinks much faster than a human being that can be programmed to do our thinking jobs.

Machine Learning is progressing rapidly, and automation is sweeping through the various job domains already. From here on, the number of jobs to be filled by people will shrink, even sophisticated roles of highly educated people.

We are witnessing a massive expansion of the ‘brain size’ of our civilisation. We will be able to leave all that hard thinking to the AIs. In fact, let’s leave all the work to the AIs.

I for one look forward to a society where we focus on just having fun, safe, happy lives, playing with our advanced technological toys.

The Last challenge

By Robbe Nagel, Student from Jeroen Bosch College

Unlike many others, I don’t necessarily expect an apocalyptic future of AI.

We will have to be very careful in the development of it however. Not doing so could result in a superintelligent AI with bad intentions towards people.

I love the “Ant example”. We don’t have anything against ants, but if they are in our way, we will destroy them. The same will happen if AI is not co-working with humans.

The benefits of AI can be very helpful. If we build a co-working relationship with AI, we will definately have an intelligence explosion. Within weeks the AI can do think-work that would take us millions of years.

No matter if AI turns out good or bad, it will be the last challenge we will ever face.
